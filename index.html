Questions, answered

What is Uchi?

Uchi (うち — "our home") is an iPhone app that turns household chores into manga trading cards. Every card is a real job with one owner, a clear definition of done, and weekly or monthly quests that roll over automatically. Learn more →

How does Uchi help couples stop arguing about chores?

It removes the three things chore fights are made of: unclear ownership (every card has one name), unclear standards (the Done-Right Bar lives on the card), and disputed memory (the Review tab keeps receipts of who did what). Learn more →

What is the mental load, and does Uchi track it?

The mental load is the invisible work of noticing, planning, and remembering chores. Uchi writes it into every card — spot it, sort it, do it — so owning a card means owning the whole job, not just the visible part. Learn more →

Is Uchi a chore chart for adults?

Yes — without the gold stars. It replaces the fridge grid with a trading-card deck: one owner per job, automatic weekly resets, and a history you can both trust. No points-for-rewards, no parent-child dynamic. Learn more →

How is Uchi gamified without being childish?

The game is structure, not stickers: choose a manga fighter who stars in your card art, clear quests with a 済 stamp, steal a slipped card from your partner (横取り!), and win the weekly MVP crown from the receipts. Learn more →

Does my partner need an account?

No forms, no friction. One person starts the house, the other joins with a single invite code and is playing in under a minute — an email sign-in is optional and can be added later to keep your deck safe. Learn more →
