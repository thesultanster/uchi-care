How do you split chores with your partner without a fight?

Assign whole jobs, not halves. The fair way to split chores is one owner per job — including noticing it needs doing and planning it — with a definition of done you both agree on, reviewed weekly. Splitting a job "50/50" splits the responsibility to remember it, which is how the same fight restarts every week.

Why 50/50 splitting fails

When "we both do dishes" is the plan, what you’ve really created is a negotiation that reopens daily. Someone still has to notice, decide it’s time, and nag — and that someone is usually the same person. Shared tasks have no owner; no owner means the mental load lands on whoever cares more.

The one-owner method, step by step

List every recurring job in the house — Uchi ships with 26 covering kitchen, bathroom, car, errands, and money. For each: name ONE owner, write the standard ("done right" = what, by when), and set the cadence (weekly or monthly). Trade whole cards until the deck feels fair. Review the receipts weekly and re-trade cards instead of re-arguing.

What "fair" actually means

Fair isn’t equal minutes — it’s equal carrying. A card you own silently and completely is worth more than three tasks your partner has to remind you about. That’s why Uchi scores ownership, not just completion: the Review tab shows who carried what this week, and the MVP crown goes to the one who carried more.
