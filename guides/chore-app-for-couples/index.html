What makes a good chore app for couples?

A good chore app for couples does three things: it gives every task exactly one owner, it records a definition of "done" you both agreed on, and it keeps receipts of who actually did what. Points, streaks, and gold stars are for kids — for two adults, the problem is fairness and visibility, not motivation.

Why do couples need a chore app at all?

Because chores are the #1 recurring fight: 49% of couples say they argue about chores often or sometimes — ahead of money (43%). The argument is rarely about the dishes themselves. It’s about who noticed the dishes, who was supposed to care, and whose standard counts. An app fixes that only if it makes ownership explicit.

What features actually matter (and which don’t)?

Matters: one clear owner per task, the full job on the card (noticing + planning + doing), a written standard, and history you can both see. Doesn’t matter for couples: leaderboards against strangers, reward points, and chore "streaks" — none of those settle the question was this handled, and by whom?
