How do you stop arguing about chores?

You stop arguing about chores by removing the three ambiguities every chore fight is made of: who owns the job (one name, not "we"), what done means (a standard you both wrote down), and what actually happened (a record neither of you can dispute). Effort was never the problem — ambiguity is.

Why you’re really fighting (it’s not the dishes)

Chores top the list of what couples fight about — 49% argue about them often or sometimes, ahead of money at 43%. Look closely and it’s never the task: it’s "I shouldn’t have to ask," "that’s not what clean means," and "I always do this." Ownership, standards, memory. Three fights wearing one apron.

The 3-agreement fix

Agreement one: every recurring job has exactly one owner who spots, plans, and does it. Agreement two: each job has a Done-Right Bar — a sentence you both sign off on, written where you can both see it. Agreement three: the record decides history, not whoever remembers louder. Uchi puts all three on trading cards so the agreements outlive the argument.

What changes in the first two weeks

The nagging stops first — cards make asking unnecessary, because the owner’s name is on the job. Then the scorekeeping in your head quiets down, because the Review tab keeps it for you. What’s left is the actual work, which was always the easy part. Deal the deck once; stop re-negotiating your house every day.
