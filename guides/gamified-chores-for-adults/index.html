How do you make chores fun for adults?

Make the system playful, not the reward. Adults don’t clean for points — but give the same jobs trading-card art, weekly quests, a stamp that hits like a boss kill, and a legal way to steal your partner’s glory, and suddenly the house is a game you’re both playing rather than a fight you’re both losing.

Why point systems fall flat after two weeks

Points borrow motivation from novelty, and novelty expires. Worse, points-for-rewards frames chores as favors to be paid out, which is exactly the dynamic adults are trying to escape. The lasting fun in games comes from ownership, mastery, and a little rivalry — not from the score counter.

The Uchi loop: claim, clear, steal

Every job is a manga card with one owner — you literally choose a fighter (a ghost, a tanuki, a mop-wielding ronin, or a manga version of yourself from one selfie) who stars in your card art. Clear your weekly quests and stamp them 済. Let one slip, and your partner can steal the clear and take the credit. 横取り! The rivalry does what reminders never could.

The part that stays fun: the receipts

Uchi’s Review tab crowns a weekly MVP from what actually got done. That tiny ceremony — crown, receipts, a new week dealt automatically — is the sustainable game loop: low effort, real stakes (bragging rights), and zero admin, because the quests reset themselves.

Put it on cards

Uchi ships the whole system — 26 cards, one owner each, weekly quests, receipts — as a free iPhone app. Your partner joins with one code, no account needed.
